How do heights compare in different parts of the country?


Each dot represents one person who reported their height in a national survey. We're sampling people one at a time and updating our estimate of the average height on each side of the line as we go, shown in the bottom plot. The error bars represent uncertainty in estimating these averages.

As we sample more and more people, our estimates settle down and our uncertainty decreases. Eventually we'll have enough data to conclude that there's a statistically significant difference, meaning that it's unlikely that we would see these differences if the true average height on each side of the line were identical.

So far we have sampled 0 people and do not have statistical significance.
